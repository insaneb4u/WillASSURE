What is a will

A testamentary declaration is called a will. It is one of the most important part of estate planning. It intimates the desire of the testator as to how he wishes to distribute his/ her after his death. An essential document that completes financial planning. Assuring security to the testator, as the testator’s intentions are executed, and his loved ones are protected. ‘Will assure” provides these services online to accomplish your estate planning in an easy, economical manner irrespective of your geographical location. Importance of the will

Wills is only way in which a person can distribute his wealth as per his desire.

  • Bequeath to a stranger
    One can give away his/her property even to a stranger easily through this document.
  • Bequeath of emotional, sentimental things Things [items] having emotional and sentimental value can be distributed to persons who understand the importance of such items, value it and appreciate them.
  • Sufficient provision for beloveds
    One can also make sufficient provisions for their beloved ones.
  • Appointment of Gaurdian
    Minor children can be entrusted to a guardian, appropriate and trusted by the testator.
  • Arrangement of Funeral expenses
    One’s funeral expenses can be provided for in a will.
  • Distribution as per desire
    One can be rest assured that after them the distribution of their wealth shall take place in the manner as per their desire.
  • Appointment of executor
    One can also appoint a trusted person as an executor.
  • Eases the process of distribution of wealth.
    For the close relatives, a will reduces the agony of the tedious process and eases the process of distribution of wealth. In case a will is not made the distribution of wealth will be as per the succession laws applicable to such person. In such an event, often the distribution of wealth may be against the wishes of the deceased person and may go to individuals to whom one does not wish to bequeath.
  • Minimal cost
    Moreover, a will can be made without incurring a major amount of money.
  • Easy to make
  • Easy to understand
    A will is made in a simple and easy language, due to which any common man can understand it and execute it smoothly
  • Avoids family disputes
    A will reduces disputes that could arise at the time of disposition reducing the mental agony and trauma that one goes through. Family do not have to face the legal complications.
  • Can controls the bequeath of assets
  • Can avail of Tax Benefits
    NRIs can make separate will for property owned in in India and avail of tax benefits.
